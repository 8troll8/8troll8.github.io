What are the issues revolving clothing waste?:

Clothing waste is the quick discarding of clothing material primarily to purchase more. This phenomenon is also known as "Fast Fashion" and can major negative impacts such as Greenhouse-Gas emissions, water consumption, overconsumption and negative social consequences.

A deeper look at the issues revolving clothing waste

  1. Greenhouse-Gas Emissions It is estimated that the fast fashion industry is responsible for 4 percent of global greenhouse gas emissions and the figure is only projected to go up. By 2030, emissions from textile manufacturing are expected to be closer to 60 percent (UN Framework Convention on Climate Change).
  2. Water Consumption and Pollution Fashion is a massive consumer of water as it takes around 700 gallons of water to produce only one cotton shirt. Substances used in the production of clothing such as dyes or the pesticides used to grow cotton can leak into waterways and lead to health issues.
  3. Overconsumption The low price of clothing on fast fashion sights is a double edged sword. It allows low income consumers to buy new products when they may not have been able to do so from higher end stores. However, these prices have also led to skyrocketing consumption levels. The constant demand for new designs causes many garments to be discarded and end up in landfills or incinerators.
  4. Social Impacts The price of fast fashion clothing is not only low because of the cheap material. Many of these companies chose to operate in developing countries where they can pay their workers low wages and benefit from loose environmental and working regulations.
